Fibre

What is Fibre?

Fibre is a broadband that connects to New Zealand’s internet network structure and provides broadband connections at the fastest speed. It has been used across New Zealand as it is the most reliable broadband connections, can function at great speeds no matter what device capacity. Fibre works by sending pulses of light through the plastic or glass cables at rapid speeds. It is the most up-to-date network of fast broadband.

Installation Process

There are a wide range of fibre install partners around New Zealand. This includes North power Fibre, Chorus, Ultra Fast Fibre, and Enable. Each install partner cover different regions of New Zealand. For example, The whole of Wellington and Lower North Island has Chorus installed Fibre, where as Christchurch has Enable. External Termination Point

The Fibre installation process begins with an external termination point (ETP) that is normally attached to the side of the building. Each building has it’s own cable Connecting the ETP is then determined by where the building’s phone and broadband services. he fibre cable can be added in addition to the copper cable or be installed to replace the copper cable which is used for traditional internet usage.
